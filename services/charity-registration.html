Charity Registration in Glasgow

Expert guidance to register your charity with OSCR (Office of the Scottish Charity Regulator) or the Charity Commission. We handle the paperwork so you can focus on your cause.

Start Your Charity the Right Way

Registering a charity in Scotland requires approval from OSCR. The application process can be complex, with specific requirements for charitable purposes, governance documents, and trustee arrangements. We guide you through every step to ensure successful registration.

Our Charity Registration Service

  • Eligibility assessment – Confirm your purposes qualify as charitable
  • Structure advice – SCIO, trust, company limited by guarantee
  • Governing documents – Constitution or articles preparation
  • OSCR application – Complete application submission
  • Query handling – Respond to OSCR questions on your behalf
  • Post-registration setup – Bank accounts, Gift Aid, ongoing compliance

Charity Structures in Scotland

  • SCIO – Scottish Charitable Incorporated Organisation (most popular)
  • Charitable company – Company limited by guarantee with charitable status
  • Unincorporated association – Simple structure for smaller charities
  • Charitable trust – Traditional trust structure

Benefits of Charity Status

  • Tax relief – Exemption from Corporation Tax and other taxes
  • Gift Aid – Reclaim tax on donations from UK taxpayers
  • Business rates relief – Up to 80% mandatory relief
  • Credibility – Enhanced trust from donors and funders
  • Grant access – Eligibility for charitable grants and funding
